Anand Karaj

Timing & Atmosphere: Formal Sikh wedding at a Gurudwara; spiritual, modest, and solemn. Color Themes: Respectful yet celebratory shades—pastels, muted jewel tones, subtle gold accents. Avoid white and black (white is for mourning, black is generally considered inauspicious). Red is reserved for the bride. Outfit Styles for Women: Traditional and modest: salwar kameez, Anarkalis, or lehengas in pastel pink, lavender, peach, or muted aqua. Dupatta or chunni required to cover the head inside Gurudwara. Outfit Styles for Men: Sherwani, bandhgala, or kurta-pajama in pastel, cream, or muted royal colors; turban or safa to cover head inside Gurudwara. Accessories & Tips: Minimal but elegant jewelry; avoid low necklines or revealing attire; shoes must be removed inside the Gurudwara; opt for lightweight socks if needed.
